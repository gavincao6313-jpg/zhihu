    3.  **Iterate:** Take the remaining uncleaned data, re-categorize, and refine the workflow.

*   **Corporate AI "Hackathons" are Often Failures:** Many large companies launch "All-in AI" hackathons or internal competitions without clear KPIs.
    *   **Result:** "Chicken feathers" (mess/failure).
    *   **Reason:** Leadership (No. 1 positions) lacks frontline feel and doesn't want to learn AI deeply. They use hackathons as "risk-free arbitrage"—if it works, they take credit; if it fails, they blame the organization or fire a few people ("kill two generals to appease the army").
    *   **Requirement:** AI transformation must be a **"No. 1 Project"** (led by BU Head/VP level), not just a grassroots initiative.
*   **Opportunity for Individuals:** When corporate hackathons fail and leadership realizes they need real results, they will start hiring (Headcount opens up). This is the window for skilled individuals to enter as experts/consultants.
